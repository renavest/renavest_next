## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 Personal Data**: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including but not limited to:
- Employee names, email addresses, and contact information
- Employment details and department affiliations
- Financial therapy usage patterns and preferences
- Session attendance and scheduling information
- Payment and billing information related to employer-sponsored benefits

**1.2 Special Categories of Personal Data**: Personal data revealing:
- Mental health information through therapy interactions
- Financial wellness assessments and therapeutic interventions
- Health-related data collected during therapy sessions

**1.3 Data Subject**: Employees of the Data Controller who access Renavest's financial therapy services through employer-sponsored benefits.

**1.4 Processing**: Any operation performed on personal data, including:
- Collection of employee therapy preferences
- Storage of session scheduling and attendance data
- Analysis for aggregate reporting to employers
- Transmission of de-identified utilization statistics

---

## 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

### 2.1 Purpose Limitation
Renavest shall process Personal Data solely for the following purposes:

**Primary Purposes:**
- Provision of financial therapy services to Data Controller's employees
- Matching employees with qualified financial therapists
- Facilitating therapy session scheduling and communication
- Processing payments and managing employer-sponsored subsidies
- Providing secure chat and video communication platforms

**Secondary Purposes:**
- Generating de-identified, aggregate utilization reports for Data Controller
- Ensuring compliance with applicable healthcare and financial regulations
- Improving service quality and user experience
- Maintaining security and preventing fraud

### 2.2 Categories of Data Processed

**Employee Identification Data:**
- Name, email address, employee ID
- Department/team affiliation
- Employment status and eligibility for benefits

**Therapy Service Data:**
- Session scheduling and attendance records
- Therapist assignments and preferences
- Communication logs (encrypted)
- Payment and billing information

**Aggregate Analytics Data:**
- De-identified usage patterns by department
- Session completion rates and satisfaction scores
- General therapy topic categories (without specific content)

**Excluded Data:**
- Specific therapy session content and clinical notes
- Individual mental health diagnoses or assessments
- Personal financial information beyond billing

---

## 3. DATA CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Lawful Basis and Employee Consent

**3.1.1** Data Controller warrants that it has established a lawful basis for processing under applicable data protection laws, including:
- Employee consent for employer-sponsored therapy benefits
- Legitimate interest in providing employee wellness programs
- Compliance with employment law obligations

**3.1.2** Data Controller shall:
- Obtain explicit, informed consent from employees before enrollment
- Provide clear privacy notices regarding data sharing with Renavest
- Ensure employees understand their rights regarding personal data
- Maintain records of consent and withdrawal requests

### 3.2 Employee Rights Management

**3.2.1** Data Controller shall inform employees of their rights, including:
- Right to access their personal data processed by Renavest
- Right to rectify inaccurate information
- Right to request deletion of personal data (subject to legal obligations)
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing or restrict processing

**3.2.2** Data Controller shall coordinate with Renavest to facilitate employee rights requests within applicable timeframes.

### 3.3 Data Minimization

**3.3.1** Data Controller shall only share personal data that is necessary for Renavest to provide the agreed services.

**3.3.2** Data Controller shall regularly review the categories of data shared and request deletion of unnecessary data.

---

## 4. DATA PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Processing Instructions

**4.1.1** Renavest shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Data Controller, including:
- Initial enrollment and eligibility verification
- Session scheduling and therapist matching
- Generation of aggregate utilization reports
- Processing of employee data access or deletion requests

**4.1.2** Renavest shall immediately inform Data Controller if instructions appear to violate applicable data protection laws.

### 4.2 Confidentiality and Security

**4.2.1** All Renavest personnel with access to Personal Data shall be bound by confidentiality obligations.

**4.2.2** Renavest shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- End-to-end encryption for all data transmissions
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- Multi-factor authentication for all system access
- Regular security patches and vulnerability assessments
- Secure backup and disaster recovery procedures

**Organizational Measures:**
- Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel
- Regular security awareness training for all staff
- Background checks for personnel with data access
- Incident response and breach notification procedures
- Annual third-party security audits

### 4.3 Sub-processor Management

**4.3.1** Renavest may engage the following categories of sub-processors:
- Cloud infrastructure providers (AWS)
- Payment processing services (Stripe)
- Authentication services (Clerk)
- Communication services (email, messaging)

**4.3.2** Current approved sub-processors:
- Amazon Web Services (AWS) - Infrastructure hosting
- Stripe, Inc. - Payment processing
- Clerk, Inc. - Authentication services
- Resend, Inc. - Email communications

**4.3.3** Renavest shall:
- Execute appropriate data processing agreements with all sub-processors
- Ensure sub-processors provide adequate security guarantees
- Notify Data Controller of any changes to sub-processors with 30 days' notice
- Remain fully liable for sub-processor compliance

### 4.4 Employee Privacy Protections

**4.4.1** Employer Reporting Restrictions:
- Only aggregate, de-identified data may be shared with Data Controller
- Reports must include minimum 5 employees per data group
- No individual employee therapy content or specific details
- No personally identifiable information in utilization reports

**4.4.2** Therapy Confidentiality:
- Individual therapy sessions remain confidential between employee and therapist
- Clinical notes and session content are not accessible to employer
- Chat communications are encrypted and protected from employer access
- Therapist-patient privilege is maintained per applicable licensing requirements

## 5. DATA TRANSFERS

### 5.1 Geographic Processing

**5.1.1** Personal Data will be processed primarily within:
- United States (primary data centers)
- Canada (backup and disaster recovery, if applicable)

**5.1.2** Any international transfers shall comply with applicable data protection laws, including adequate protection mechanisms such as:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Adequacy decisions where applicable

### 5.2 Employer Access Restrictions

**5.2.1** Data Controller acknowledges and agrees that:
- Individual employee therapy data shall not be accessible to employer personnel
- Employer access is limited to aggregate, de-identified reports only
- Employee consent is required for any data sharing beyond standard reporting
- Therapist-patient confidentiality supersedes employer data access rights

---

## 6. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

### 6.1 Retention Periods

**Active Employees:**
- Personal data retained during employment and benefit eligibility
- Session data retained per applicable healthcare record requirements
- Communication logs retained for 7 years for compliance purposes

**Terminated Employees:**
- Personal data deletion within 90 days of benefit termination (unless legal hold)
- Clinical data retention per therapist licensing requirements
- Financial records retained per applicable tax and compliance requirements

### 6.2 Data Deletion Procedures

**6.2.1** Upon termination of this agreement, Renavest shall:
- Securely delete or return all Personal Data within 90 days
- Provide certification of data destruction
- Ensure sub-processors also delete or return data
- Maintain minimal data only as required by law

**6.2.2** Employee-Requested Deletion:
- Individual employees may request data deletion through Renavest platform
- Deletion completed within 30 days (subject to legal obligations)
- Employer notification of deletion requests (without identifying specifics)

---

## 7. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 7.1 Incident Response

**7.1.1** Renavest shall notify Data Controller of any personal data breach within **24 hours** of discovery, including:
- Nature and scope of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
- Likely consequences and measures taken to address the breach
- Contact point for further information

**7.1.2** Renavest shall provide regular updates on investigation and remediation efforts.

### 7.2 Regulatory Notification Support

**7.2.1** Renavest shall assist Data Controller in:
- Determining regulatory notification requirements
- Preparing breach notifications to supervisory authorities
- Notifying affected employees when required
- Coordinating with law enforcement if necessary

---

## 8. COMPLIANCE AND AUDITING

### 8.1 Compliance Frameworks

**8.1.1** Renavest shall maintain compliance with:
- HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
- SOC 2 Type II security standards
- Applicable state data protection laws
- Financial services regulations

### 8.2 Audit Rights

**8.2.1** Data Controller may:
- Request annual compliance reports and certifications
- Conduct audits with 30 days' written notice
- Review security policies and procedures
- Verify compliance with this DPA

**8.2.2** Audit costs shall be borne by Data Controller unless material non-compliance is discovered.

## 9. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 Limitation of Liability

**9.1.1** Each party's liability under this DPA shall be limited as set forth in the main service agreement.

**9.1.2** Renavest shall maintain appropriate cyber liability insurance covering data protection obligations.

### 9.2 Indemnification

**9.2.1** Renavest shall indemnify Data Controller against claims arising from:
- Renavest's violation of this DPA
- Unauthorized processing by Renavest or its sub-processors
- Security breaches caused by Renavest's negligence

**9.2.2** Data Controller shall indemnify Renavest against claims arising from:
- Lack of proper employee consent
- Violation of employment laws
- Instructions that violate applicable data protection laws

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 10.1 Term
This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the main service agreement between the parties.

### 10.2 Termination Rights
Either party may terminate this DPA upon:
- Material breach of data protection obligations
- Termination of the main service agreement
- 30 days' written notice for convenience

### 10.3 Post-Termination Obligations
Upon termination, both parties shall:
- Cease processing Personal Data (except as required by law)
- Return or securely delete all Personal Data
- Ensure sub-processors comply with deletion requirements
